Kitbull

Kitbull is a story about a kitten that lives in a cardboard box that sits on top of a pile of garbage in a San Francisco junkyard and a puppy that lives in the building next door. The kitten is initially very scared of the puppy but connects with him over time, experience, and empathy. Kitbull is a tale of two friends working together toward a happy ending.

Production Details

Kitbull is a traditionally animated short film that was produced in January 2019 and premiered at El Capitan Theater in Hollywood on January 18 and released to the public on Pixar’s YouTube channel on February 18. Kitbull was written and directed directed by Rosana Sullivan and is the third short created under Pixar’s Production and Animation Studio’s “SparkShorts” program.

Inspiration & Creation

The short was inspired by cat video binge. Writer Rosana Sullivan admits to finding cat videos helpful during times of stress and says that while she had initially wished to “draw something that made her feel good, it evolved into something more” to her in the end. The action of Kitbull takes place in the Mission District, which is the first place Sullivan lived after moving to San Francisco, so it holds a special place in her heart.

Though the 2D style of this short is different from what Pixar normally does, the personal touches make it feel very much like a Pixar product. Every frame is hand-drawn and hand-painted and the backgrounds were created by blending impressionistic, loose and fast painting with a bit of grunge and grit. Sullivan also says that the kitten’s nervous personality was made known in the short in the way she “uses a lot of the setting, like street signs and shadows, to stay invisible.” She highlighted the personal importance of this short and can’t wait for viewers to meet the little kitten.
